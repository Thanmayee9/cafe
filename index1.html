Prep Techniques

Coffee beans are ground to the desired fineness — typically a fine grind for espresso.The grounds are packed (tamped) into a portafilter to ensure even extraction.

Extracting

The portafilter is inserted into the espresso machine, and hot pressurized water is forced through the grounds.

Serving

The espresso is dispensed directly into a cup, which is usually pre-warmed for the best experience.

The Steamy Scoop

The “steamy” evokes the hot, fragrant steam released as coffee is brewed,

capturing the warmth and inviting nature of a fresh cup.

Expert brewing

A “scoop” refers to the measured portion of ground coffee, suggesting precision in the craft of brewing.



Rich extraction

In brewing methods like espresso, hot water under pressure extracts bold flavors and oils, producing concentrated, flavorful shots topped with crema—the creamy golden layer prized by coffee lovers.



Variety of brewing styles

Whether it’s espresso, drip, or specialty brews (such as siphon or moka pot), “Steamy Scoop” conjures artisanal coffee preparation that carefully balances water temperature, grind size, and extraction time for optimal flavor.
